Pre-Zen Studies: Pre-Zen Coaly Nancy Nine Chews Ol'.

2011-04-04

Generations of scholars of the origins of Zen have treated the imperturbable calmness of the Patsmōka Shasta, the eccentric style of the Zūt Sūtra, the belligerence of the Legoa Maitreya Ijit, and the detached insouciance of the Aditt' Amytabhaha. Joshu Wa Koan analyzed the influence of Judaism in Zen. Finding nothing to add to their work, this writer decided to study instead the well-known but little-understood chant about Zen's precursors, Pre-Zen Coaly Nancy Nine Chews Ol'. (The song has elsewhere been referred to elsewhere under the cryptic name of "Prisencolinensinainciusol".)

Through repeated careful listening, the author has established that the received text of the chant is incorrect. Not only is it written in Italian orthography (absurd for a text in English), it is also full of outright errors. A corrected text is included below, and meaning now begins to appear in some parts of the text.

The text displays a mixture of dialects. Some passages reflect an Italian accent, whereas others display American accents. This calls for further study.

Even corrected, some passages remain hard to interpret. For instance, should we understand "Yeah, push! yo-ho", a simple(?) expression of muscular effort, or "Yeah, push yo' ho'," hinting at mysogyny, abuse, and racism?

The next step should be to ask the principal transmitter of this work, Adriano Celentano, to critique this text so a proper critical edition can be published. The author will be grateful to anyone that can put him contact with that great scholar.

-- Richard Stallman
